ECMAtlas, An Introduction...

The extracellular matrix (ECM) is composed of approximately 200 proteins that form the structural scaffolding for most tissues.

In addition to defining many of the physical characteristics of a tissue, the ECM provides storage and regulation of signaling molecules, and biochemical queues through Cell-ECM contacts.

These interactions support organ homeostasis, or conversely, influence disease progression. Despite the importance of the ECM in health and disease our understanding of ECM biology pales in comparison to most classes of intracellular proteins.
